# Scientific Literature Synthesis for "eek-a-volve": Evolutionary, Ecological, Cognitive, Cultural and Artificial-Life Mechanisms as a Coupled Network

## 1. Executive Summary

The most scientifically defensible account of eek-a-volve is that its systems form a **densely coupled feedback network dominated by negative, stabilising, and non-monotonic relationships**, not a collection of positively reinforcing modules. The single most important finding for the design is that **greater cognitive ability, technology, and complexity are NOT universally favoured**: each is bounded by energetic, demographic, and ecological conditions, and each is demonstrably reversible. This is well supported [established].

Well-supported couplings (suitable to bake in): the metabolism↔cognition energetic trade-off (expensive-tissue/expensive-brain); predation→grouping (dilution, many-eyes, selfish-herd); group size/density→disease transmission; the virulence↔transmission trade-off; transmission-fidelity→cumulative culture; culture→biological selection (lactase persistence); niche construction→modified selection with ecological inheritance. Contested couplings (model with competing positions): parasite-mediated sexual selection (Hamilton–Zuk); the social-brain hypothesis as the driver of brain size; whether group size per se (vs contact structure) predicts parasitism; the 3/4 metabolic exponent. Speculative couplings (label as such, do not assume): the emergence of cumulative culture, technology, civilisation, and AGI-like cognition from lower-level traits; artificial-agent couplings to civilisation stability.

Principal scientific risks: (1) treating intelligence as a monotonic good; (2) treating technology/civilisation as inevitable once prerequisites exist; (3) treating complexity as irreversible; (4) labelling capacities "emergent" that in practice require modeller scaffolding. Each is contradicted by the evidence below.

## 4. Open-Endedness and Emergence Synthesis

**The major-transitions framework.** Szathmáry & Maynard Smith (1995, *Nature* 374:227–232) define the major transitions by a single diagnostic: entities that could replicate independently before the transition can replicate only as part of a larger whole afterwards, accompanied by division of labour and new means of information storage/transmission (culminating in language) [established]. They explicitly reject a built-in drive toward complexity: "there is no theoretical reason to expect evolutionary lineages to increase in complexity with time, and no empirical evidence that they do so." Szathmáry's 2015 update (*PNAS* 112:10104–10111) distinguishes *fraternal* (like units, e.g. cells→multicellular) from *egalitarian* (unlike units, e.g. endosymbiosis) transitions, decomposes each into origin/maintenance/transformation phases, makes multilevel selection central, and stresses that transitions are contingent, not inevitable, and that "not all transitions possess all common features." This directly supports treating eek-a-volve transitions as conditional and non-mandatory.

**Per-transition emergence verdict:**

- **Multicellularity / transitions in individuality:** Emergence is *demonstrated but usually scaffolded* in silico. Digital systems (DISHTINY, Moreno & Ofria 2019) evolve fraternal transitions, but only after the modeller provides hereditary grouping mechanisms and incentives; the "ecological scaffolding" account (Black et al. 2020, *Proc R Soc B*) argues the environment must impose collective-level birth–death for individuality to arise. Verdict: **plausible with scaffolding; not spontaneous** [debated].
- **Division of labour:** Demonstrated only once grouping + differential reproduction are scaffolded (Goldsby et al.; DISHTINY) [debated].
- **Social learning:** Emerges in RL/ALife when memory, perception of conspecifics, and payoff to copying are pre-built; not from generic agents [debated].
- **Cumulative culture / cultural ratchet:** No unambiguous case of open-ended cumulative culture emerging spontaneously in an artificial system without designed high-fidelity transmission. Human-uniqueness arguments (Tomasello 1999; Tennie et al. 2009 "zone of latent solutions") imply this is the hardest transition to get for free. Verdict: **typically scaffolded** [debated]/[speculative-extrapolation].
- **Tool use:** Baker et al. 2019 (OpenAI hide-and-seek) show genuinely emergent multi-stage tool use (6 phases) from multi-agent competition + scale — but the authors caution the "strategy space in this environment is inherently bounded and likely will not surpass the six modes." Verdict: **demonstrated within a bounded space; open-endedness not achieved** [debated].

**Open-endedness as an unsolved problem.** ALife reviews (OEE workshops; Soros & Stanley "necessary conditions"; Packard/Bedau) treat the spontaneous, indefinite generation of qualitatively new complexity — including recurring major transitions — as an *open, unsolved* problem; some authors (Beslon) conjecture biology itself is not open-ended with respect to ever-higher individuality. **Design implication:** any "emergent" label in eek-a-volve should be reserved for capacities that arise from lower-level traits without a designed detector; capacities triggered at a designed threshold should be labelled [design-abstraction], not emergent.

**Reversibility and loss.** The literature strongly supports reversibility, which the simulator requires. (1) Cultural-knowledge loss: Henrich 2004 (Tasmania) models adaptive social learning producing *maladaptive* skill loss when effective population falls below a critical size (Tasmania ~5000→2000, with loss of bone tools, cold-weather clothing, hafted tools, nets, fishing/barbed spears, spear-throwers, boomerangs). (2) Societal collapse: Tainter 1988 frames collapse as loss of socio-political complexity from declining marginal returns; Butzer & Endfield 2012 (*PNAS* 109:3628–3631) find 7 of 12 stressed societies severely transformed while 5 recovered via environmental, political, or socio-cultural resilience — collapse is neither uniform nor terminal. (3) Demographic collapse-and-recovery: evolutionary rescue (Gomulkiewicz & Holt 1995; Bell & Gonzalez 2009) produces a characteristic U-shaped abundance trajectory — decline, trough, genetic recovery — within ~25 generations in yeast, conditional on a population-size threshold and standing variation. All three support modelling civilisation/complexity as a reversible state rather than an absorbing one [established for the demographic/cultural cases; debated for societal-collapse mechanism].

## 5. Literature-Reported Parameter Values (as published; not converted to simulator units)

- **Metabolic scaling exponent:** Kleiber ~0.75 (M^3/4); contested alternatives 2/3 (surface-area/Rubner) and up to ~1 (isometric) for small/unicellular organisms; WBE 1997 derive 3/4 from fractal distribution networks but critics (Apol et al. 2008; Kozłowski) show the derivation yields a single vessel or isometry under realistic assumptions. Treat exponent as a tunable parameter, not a constant [debated].
- **Neural energetic fraction:** Human brain ~2% of body mass, ~20% (cited range ~20–25%) of resting metabolic rate; neural tissue ≈22× the mass-specific metabolic cost of skeletal muscle; brain + splanchnic + heart + kidneys ≈60–70% of RMR at <6% of body mass (Aiello & Wheeler 1995; Gallagher et al. 1998) [established].
- **R0 and density-dependence:** Measles R0 is often cited as 12–18 (Anderson & May), but the 2017 systematic review by Guerra et al. (*Lancet Infect Dis* 17:e420–e428) "identified feasible measles R0 values of 3.7–203.3," emphasising strong context dependence; seasonal influenza ~1.2–2.5; Ebola ~1.5–2.5; COVID-19 early ~2–4. Transmission modelled as density-dependent (βSI) or frequency-dependent (βSI/N); the choice sets the group-size threshold (Anderson & May 1979/1991) [established].
- **Virulence–transmission trade-off shape:** Acevedo et al. 2019 (*Evolution* 73:636) screened "over 6800 studies" and meta-analysed 29, finding strong support for replication→virulence and replication→transmission being increasing; whether they decelerate (giving an intermediate optimum) is uncertain. Alizon et al. 2009 (*J. Evol. Biol.* 22:245–259) is a narrative review concluding "trade-offs exist, but may not be of the simple form" [established for sign; debated for curvature].
- **Cultural transmission fidelity:** Modelling shows trait longevity increases approximately exponentially with copying fidelity, so small fidelity gains greatly increase the number/complexity of maintained traits (Enquist et al.; Lewis & Laland 2012) [established as model result].
- **Strength of culturally driven selection:** Bersaglieri et al. 2004 estimate the lactase-persistence selection coefficient "between 0.014 and 0.15 for the CEPH population and between 0.09 and 0.19" for Scandinavian trios — "comparable to the strongest selection" yet observed genome-wide; the LCT −13,910*T derived allele is <10 kyr old [established].
- **Critical population size for culture:** Below a critical effective size, expected skill complexity declines (Henrich 2004); Tasmania reconstruction ~5000→2000 associated with documented technology loss [debated].
- **Mutation rate orders of magnitude:** (Reported from general genetics literature; not re-derived here) point-mutation rates per base per generation typically 10^-8–10^-9 in many eukaryotes, with per-genome per-replication rates near ~0.003 in microbes (Drake's rule) — orders of magnitude only. Flag: not directly searched in this run; verify against Drake et al. 1998 before use [established].

## 6. Failure-Mode Evidence (claims the simulator must AVOID baking in)

1. **"Intelligence is universally adaptive."** Contradicted. Heldstab/Isler et al. (2022, "economics of brain size") conclude cognitive abilities drive brain enlargement only where they improve net energy balance under favourable ecological preconditions; otherwise the metabolic cost is not repaid. Brain size has *decreased* in many domesticated lineages and in late-Pleistocene humans. Within-family human studies find near-zero brain-size↔cognition correlation. **Condition for the claim to hold:** only when cognition yields ecological/social energy returns exceeding its developmental, metabolic, and life-history costs [established that it is bounded].
2. **"Technology/civilisation inevitably follow once prerequisites exist."** Contradicted. Cumulative culture requires high-fidelity transmission and sufficient population size/connectedness (Tomasello; Henrich 2004; Powell et al. 2009); the same adaptive processes can produce loss. Major-transitions theory denies any inherent drive to complexity (Szathmáry & Maynard Smith 1995). **Condition:** prerequisites are necessary but not sufficient; demography and transmission structure gate the outcome.
3. **"Complexity is irreversible."** Contradicted. Cultural loss (Tasmania), societal collapse (Tainter 1988; Butzer & Endfield 2012, with 7/12 transformed and 5/12 recovered), and demographic collapse-and-rescue (Gomulkiewicz & Holt 1995; Bell & Gonzalez 2009) all show complexity is routinely lost and sometimes regained. **Condition:** reversibility is the empirically supported default; design states as reversible.
4. **"Emergent capacities arise for free."** Contradicted by ALife practice: transitions to multicellularity, division of labour, and cumulative culture are typically scaffolded by the modeller; spontaneous open-ended emergence remains unsolved (OEE literature). **Condition:** label threshold-triggered capacities [design-abstraction].

## Caveats

- Search budget was exhausted before retrieving major-transitions, life-history, and evolutionary-rescue primary sources directly; those entries were obtained via a focused subagent and should be re-verified against publisher pages before publication.
- All extrapolations from biological theory to eek-a-volve design are labelled [design-abstraction] or [speculative-extrapolation]; artificial-agent↔civilisation couplings (#36) have essentially no biological evidential base and are the most speculative content here.
- Where a theory is contested (Hamilton–Zuk parasite-mediated sexual selection, the social-brain hypothesis as driver of brain size, the 3/4 scaling exponent, niche construction as a distinct evolutionary "force"), both positions are presented rather than a single verdict.
- The virulence-trade-off meta-analysis figure (>6800 studies screened) belongs to Acevedo et al. 2019, not Alizon et al. 2009 (a narrative review); this was corrected during enrichment. Earlier internal drafts had mis-attributed it.
- Several secondary sources surfaced in search (Wikipedia, blogs, GRIN/ResearchGate uploads) were used only to locate primary literature, not as evidence; no claim above rests on them.
- Mutation-rate orders of magnitude (§5) were not directly searched in this run and should be verified against Drake et al. 1998 (*Genetics* 148:1667) before use.

## Recommendations (staged, with thresholds that would change them)

**Stage 1 — Bake in only the well-supported, energy/behaviour/hazard/reproduction-expressible couplings.** Implement metabolism↔cognition (energy drain proportional to controller size, repaid only via foraging/predation energy returns — coupling #1–3), predation→grouping via per-capita hazard reduction (#4–5), density→disease transmission (#6, density-dependent βSI), the virulence↔transmission trade-off as an intermediate-optimum parasite trait (#8–9), and transmission-fidelity-gated cumulative culture (#11). These map cleanly onto the four permitted channels and are [established]. *Threshold to revise:* if agent counts force per-pair contact computation to dominate runtime, switch disease to frequency-dependent transmission (βSI/N) and grouping to local-neighbourhood approximations.

**Stage 2 — Implement contested couplings as toggleable, parameterised, and explicitly bracketed.** Parasite-mediated sexual selection (#20), the social-brain pathway (#18), and the metabolic exponent (#21) should be options with both signs/strengths available, defaulting to the more conservative setting, because the evidence is genuinely mixed. *Threshold:* treat any of these as "core" only if a within-simulator ablation shows the qualitative target behaviour depends on it.

**Stage 3 — Treat all emergence and civilisation/AGI content as detection-at-threshold [design-abstraction], not spontaneous emergence.** Because open-ended evolution is unsolved and major transitions are typically scaffolded in silico, eek-a-volve should (a) provide the scaffolding (hereditary grouping, payoff to copying, high-fidelity channel) if these transitions are wanted, and (b) honestly label any capacity that is triggered at a designed threshold as a design abstraction, reserving "emergent" for capacities verified to arise without a detector. *Threshold to upgrade a label to "emergent":* the capacity reproducibly appears across seeds from lower-level traits with the detector disabled.

**Stage 4 — Make complexity reversible by construction.** Implement cultural-knowledge loss below a critical effective population size (#30), demographic collapse-and-rescue with the U-shaped signature (#24), and civilisation as a non-absorbing state (#32–34). The evidence (Henrich; Gomulkiewicz & Holt; Bell & Gonzalez; Tainter; Butzer & Endfield) makes reversibility the empirically supported default. *Threshold:* if any state becomes a de facto attractor (no observed exits across long runs), add an explicit decay/degradation hazard rather than leaving it terminal.

**Overarching benchmark:** the simulator should be judged scientifically honest if, across runs, intelligence, technology, and civilisation each sometimes fail to appear and sometimes regress — matching the bounded, non-monotonic, reversible picture the literature supports. If any of these rises monotonically and irreversibly by default, a modelling assumption — not a biological law — is driving it.
